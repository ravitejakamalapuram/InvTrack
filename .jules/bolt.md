## 2025-02-12 - Replaced .where().toList() with for loop
**Learning:** In Dart, replacing chained `Iterable` methods like `.where().toList()` with a standard `for` loop directly avoids closure allocation per element and prevents the creation of intermediate lists. This is a noticeable micro-optimization that can lead to measurable performance improvement when parsing through large numbers of items in Riverpod providers.
**Action:** Always favor basic loop constructs over chained Iterables for data-heavy manipulation in tight operations (e.g. data grouping, stream processing) unless readability is highly prioritized over a slight execution speedup. Also, ensure golden test failures generated by running `flutter test` are cleared so as not to pollute the commit.
